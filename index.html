Bugsnag
At Bugsnag, I explored the intersection of design and engineering. Learning how to create usable prototypes with React, Javascript, and SCSS was a great addition to my repertoire.
My favorite project at Bugsnag was working on Settings 2.0. During that time, my main responsibilities were managing concept art and the layout of the Settings page.
Bugsnag is an error monitoring dashboard that allows you to see what's wrong with your application. With Bugsnag's powerful filter, you can create alerts and keep track of specific errors.

Cover
Cover came to Collective Ray with the idea to completely replace the stock Android lockscreen. We created a simple and beautiful way to access your favorite applications, based on the user's habits. Our mission was making the most visible part of Android a simple and joyful experience.
Cover is made up of 5 different modes depending on what you're doing throughout your day. This application changes according to your habits and preferences.
Many lockscreens were created before finding the proper balance between minimalism and functionality.
The final product has easy access to everything you need.

Whistle
Whistle is a dog-tracking device with a paired application. Whistle came to Collective Ray when they were still creating hardware prototypes for tracking daily activity. Together, we helped shape the future of dog wellness.
The main goal was to make checking on your dog during the day quick and pleasant. At this stage, we used Flinto for quick feedback from the client.
When we arrived at a solid place with our structure, layout, and data visualizations, it was time to explore visual direction.
After some iteration, our work was finalized and the first version of Whistle was created. Now everyone can keep track of their dog!
